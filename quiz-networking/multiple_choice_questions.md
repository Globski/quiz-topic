# Question: What is the OSI model and who developed it?

**Answer:**
The OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) model is a reference model developed by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) that provides a common basis for coordinating standards development for systems interconnection.

# Question: How many layers does the OSI model have, and what are they called?

**Answer:**
The OSI model has seven layers: Physical, Data Link, Network, Transport, Session, Presentation, and Application.

# Question: What range of communication does the OSI model describe?

**Answer:**
It describes communications from the physical transmission of bits across a medium to the highest-level data representation in a distributed application.

# Question: How do the layers in the OSI model interact?

**Answer:**
Each layer has defined functions, serving the layer above it and being served by the layer below, allowing communication through the exchange of protocol data units (PDUs) between peer layers.

# Question: What is the Internet protocol suite and how does it relate to the OSI model?

**Answer:**
The Internet protocol suite, developed contemporaneously with the OSI model and primarily funded by the U.S. Department of Defense, focuses mainly on software layers of communication and has a less rigorous structure compared to the OSI model. It was the foundation for the development of the Internet.

# Question: Why is the OSI model considered important in networking?

**Answer:**
The OSI model provides a widely accepted, user-friendly framework that standardizes and clarifies networking concepts, enabling transparent peer-to-peer communication and serving as the standard teaching model in IT.

# Question: What does peer-to-peer networking mean in the context of the OSI model?

**Answer:**
Peer-to-peer networking refers to the transparent communication between equivalent layers (peers) on two different systems through the exchange of protocol data units (PDUs).

# Question: How are computer networks commonly categorized?

**Answer:**
Computer networks are commonly categorized by their scope or scale, referred to as types of area networks, such as LAN, WAN, WLAN, MAN, SAN, CAN, and PAN.

# Question: What is the main difference between a LAN and a WAN?

**Answer:**
A LAN (Local Area Network) connects devices over a relatively short distance and is typically owned and managed by a single organization, while a WAN (Wide Area Network) spans large physical distances, connects multiple LANs, and is usually under distributed ownership.

# Question: What are common connectivity technologies used in LANs?

**Answer:**
LANs primarily use Ethernet and Token Ring technologies for connectivity.

# Question: What is the role of a router in a WAN?

**Answer:**
A router connects LANs to a WAN, maintaining both LAN and WAN IP addresses to facilitate communication across networks.

# Question: How does home networking typically use LAN and WAN?

**Answer:**
A home network usually has one LAN connecting devices locally, which accesses the internet WAN via an ISP using a broadband modem. Devices communicate within the LAN directly but must go through a central gateway (router) to reach the WAN.

# Question: What is a WLAN?

**Answer:**
A WLAN (Wireless Local Area Network) is a LAN that uses Wi-Fi wireless network technology instead of wired connections.

# Question: What is a MAN and who typically owns it?

**Answer:**
A MAN (Metropolitan Area Network) spans an area larger than a LAN but smaller than a WAN, like a city, and is typically owned and operated by a single entity such as a government or large corporation.

# Question: What is a PAN and what technology might it use?

**Answer:**
A PAN (Personal Area Network) surrounds an individual and often uses wireless technologies like Bluetooth to connect devices.

# Question: What is a Storage Area Network (SAN)?

**Answer:**
A SAN connects servers to data storage devices using technologies such as Fibre Channel.

# Question: What is a System Area Network or CAN?

**Answer:**
A System Area Network (also called Cluster Area Network or CAN) links high-performance computers with high-speed connections in a cluster configuration.

# Question: What are VPNs and EPNs in the context of networking?

**Answer:**
VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) and EPNs (Enterprise Private Networks) are types of private networks focused on secure or enterprise-level communications.

# Question: What is a Local Area Network (LAN)?

**Answer:**
A LAN is a computer network that interconnects computers within a limited area such as a residence, campus, or building, with locally managed network equipment.

# Question: What are the primary purposes of a LAN?

**Answer:**
LANs facilitate the distribution of data and sharing of network devices, such as printers, within the local area.

# Question: How does a LAN differ from a WAN?

**Answer:**
A LAN covers a limited geographic area and is locally managed, while a WAN covers a larger geographic distance and generally involves leased telecommunication circuits or Internet links.

# Question: How does a LAN compare to the Internet?

**Answer:**
The Internet is a global system connecting business and personal computers worldwide, whereas a LAN is limited to a smaller, local area.

# Question: What are the two most common technologies used in LANs today?

**Answer:**
Ethernet and Wi-Fi are the two most common technologies used for local area networks.

# Question: Name some historical network technologies used in LANs.

**Answer:**
Historical LAN technologies include ARCNET, Token Ring, and LocalTalk.

# Question: What is a Wide Area Network (WAN)?

**Answer:**
A WAN is a telecommunications network that extends over a large geographic area, often established with leased telecommunication circuits.

# Question: Who commonly uses WANs and for what purpose?

**Answer:**
Businesses, schools, and government entities use WANs to relay data among staff, students, clients, buyers, and suppliers across various locations worldwide, enabling operations regardless of location.

# Question: Can the Internet be considered a WAN?

**Answer:**
Yes, the Internet may be considered the largest example of a WAN.

# Question: How do WANs differ from LANs?

**Answer:**
WANs cover large geographic areas and can be private or public, whereas LANs refer to networks that are physically close together and locally managed.

# Question: What is the Internet?

**Answer:**
The Internet is the global system of interconnected computer networks that use the Internet protocol suite (TCP/IP) to communicate between networks and devices, forming a network of networks that includes private, public, academic, business, and government networks worldwide.

# Question: What types of information and services does the Internet carry?

**Answer:**
The Internet carries a vast range of resources and services, including the World Wide Web, electronic mail, internet telephony, file sharing, blogs, microblogging, games, instant messaging, podcasts, shopping, television, and voice over IP.

# Question: What are the historical origins of the Internet?

**Answer:**
The Internet originated from research on time-sharing computer resources and packet switching in the 1960s, developed further by DARPA in the 1970s through the ARPANET, which connected regional academic and military networks to enable resource sharing.

# Question: How did the Internet transition to its modern form?

**Answer:**
The transition began with the funding of the National Science Foundation Network in the 1980s, commercial extensions in the 1990s, the linking of commercial networks, and the advent of the World Wide Web, leading to exponential growth in connected computers and services.

# Question: How has the Internet reshaped traditional communication media?

**Answer:**
The Internet has reshaped or bypassed traditional media like telephone, radio, television, paper mail, and newspapers by enabling email, internet telephony, online music, digital newspapers, streaming, social networking, instant messaging, and online shopping.

# Question: Does the Internet have a single centralized governance?

**Answer:**
No, the Internet has no single centralized governance; each constituent network sets its own policies. However, the IP address space and Domain Name System (DNS) are overseen by ICANN, and core protocol standardization is managed by the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF).

# Question: What organizations are responsible for managing key Internet functions?

**Answer:**
ICANN manages the IP address space and DNS, while the IETF handles the technical standards and protocols that underpin the Internet.

# Question: What is a MAC address and what does it identify?

**Answer:**
A MAC (Media Access Control) address is a unique hardware identifier assigned to a network interface card (NIC) that identifies a device on a network.

# Question: How is a MAC address different from an IP address?

**Answer:**
A MAC address represents hardware and is unique to a deviceâ€™s network adapter, while an IP address represents software and identifies devices on a network at the software level.

# Question: What role does the Address Resolution Protocol (ARP) play regarding MAC addresses?

**Answer:**
ARP translates IP addresses into MAC addresses to enable data to be sent to the correct hardware device on a network.

# Question: Why are MAC addresses useful for network administrators?

**Answer:**
Because MAC addresses never change (unlike dynamic IP addresses), they provide a reliable way to identify devices on a network for diagnosing issues and controlling access.

# Question: What is MAC filtering in wireless networks?

**Answer:**
MAC filtering is a security method where a router allows network access only to devices with approved MAC addresses, blocking unauthorized devices even if they have a valid IP address.

# Question: How are MAC addresses assigned and what is an OUI?

**Answer:**
Manufacturers assign MAC addresses to network adapters during production. The Organizationally Unique Identifier (OUI) is the first part of a MAC address that identifies the manufacturer.

# Question: Give examples of OUIs for well-known manufacturers.

**Answer:**
Dell: 00-14-22
Nortel: 00-04-DC
Cisco: 00-40-96
Belkin: 00-30-BD

# Question: How is a MAC address formatted?

**Answer:**
A MAC address is usually six pairs of hexadecimal digits separated by colons or hyphens, e.g., 00:0a:95:9d:68:16.

# Question: How can you find the MAC address on a Windows 10 computer using Command Prompt?

**Answer:**
Open Command Prompt, type `ipconfig /all`, press Enter, and look for "Physical Address" under your network adapter details.

# Question: How can you find the MAC address on macOS?

**Answer:**
Go to Apple icon > System Preferences > Network > Advanced > Hardware tab, where the MAC address is listed.

# Question: How can you find the MAC address on a Linux machine?

**Answer:**
Open a terminal and run the command `ifconfig -a` to display MAC addresses for all network interfaces.

# Question: How can you find the MAC address on an iOS device?

**Answer:**
Go to Settings > General > About > Wi-Fi Address to find the MAC address.

# Question: How can you find the MAC address on an Android device?

**Answer:**
Open Settings > Network & Internet > Wi-Fi > Advanced > Network Details to see the MAC address. Note some Android devices use randomized MAC addresses for privacy.
